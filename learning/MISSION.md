# Mission

Become comfortable enough with PyTorch and neural-network fundamentals to
independently inspect, implement, modify, train, debug, and experiment with
small models—without treating the framework as magic. Build that understanding
from the MNIST implementation upward, then use it to understand transformers
and modern language models from the implementation level upward.

## Evidence of progress

- Predict a tensor's shape, dtype, and device before running code.
- Explain a forward pass and one gradient update in both NumPy and PyTorch.
- Diagnose common shape, dtype, device, and gradient-flow failures.
- Train and evaluate a small model, then make a deliberate experimental change.
- Implement a minimal attention block, then a small transformer experiment,
  after understanding the pieces.

## Scope for now

Prioritize fundamentals, experiments, and clear mental models. CNNs are useful
practice for image shapes and weight sharing but are not a gate before
transformers. Defer large-scale training, distributed systems, deployment, and
framework edge cases until they serve a project.
